The Question

Which counties face both frequent and severe pollution? We measure frequency using a county’s 5-year average Median AQI (the daily “grind”) and severity using its 5-year average Max AQI (extreme events). Counties in the top 90% for both metrics are labeled “Double Jeopardy” — places that never get a break, and when they get hit, they get hit hard.

Why identifying these counties matters

1) Triage for Public Health Resources

Residents face compounded risk: chronic exposure with no recovery time before acute spikes. Action: prioritize respiratory clinic funding, subsidized inhalers, and school/elder-care air purifiers.

2) Infrastructure & Urban Planning

Chronic pollution often reflects infrastructure/geography; acute pollution often reflects events (wildfires/industrial incidents). Action: target green buffers and heat-mitigation design in Double Jeopardy zones.

3) Real-Time Crisis Preparedness

A county with low median but high max needs seasonal readiness; Double Jeopardy counties need year-round readiness. Action: justify permanent high-precision monitoring and response protocols.

4) Economic & Insurance Policy

Climate risk is increasingly priced into health costs and housing. Action: quantify the hidden “Clean Air Tax” through higher medical bills and reduced quality of life.
